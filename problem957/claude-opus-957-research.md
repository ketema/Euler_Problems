## Incidence geometry and closure operators provide the framework

The core mathematical framework is **incidence geometry with closure operators**—the study of how points and lines generate new points through intersection rules. A closure operator cl(·) on a set of points satisfies three axioms: extensivity (A ⊆ cl(A)), monotonicity (A ⊆ B implies cl(A) ⊆ cl(B)), and idempotency (cl(cl(A)) = cl(A)). In projective planes, the closure of a point set is the smallest flat containing them, generated by taking all lines through point pairs and adding their intersection points iteratively.

**The Mac Lane-Steinitz exchange property** captures the essence of iterative point generation: if point p enters the closure when we add point q (i.e., p ∈ cl(A∪{q}) but p ∉ cl(A)), then q ∈ cl(A∪{p}). This symmetric relationship explains how geometric configurations expand—each new point enables other points to appear through line intersections, creating cascading growth.
