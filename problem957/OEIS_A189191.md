## Combinatorial Collapse Factors ($\kappa_n$)

The recurrence relation (see `MATHEMATICAL_PROOF.md`) requires $\kappa_n$ values representing the degree of coincidence at each level.

### Empirically Derived $\kappa$ Values

| Day | $P_n$ | $C_n^{\text{gen}}$ | $m_n$ | $\kappa_n$ (exact) | $\kappa_n$ (decimal) |
|-----|-------|-------------------|-------|-------------------|---------------------|
| 1 | 1 | 6 | 6 | 1 | 1.000000 |
| 2 | 27 | 162 | 20 | 10/81 | 0.123457 |
| 3 | 350 | 2,100 | 156 | 13/175 | 0.074286 |
| 4 | 16,458 | 98,748 | 1,462 | 731/49,374 | 0.014806 |
| 5 | 1,066,953 | 6,401,718 | 17,515 | ~0.002736 | 0.002736 |

**Where**:
- $P_n = \binom{m_{n-1}}{2} + m_{n-1}B_{n-2}$ (blue-pair count at max-level $n-1$)
- $C_n^{\text{gen}} = 6P_n$ (generic candidate intersection count)
- $\kappa_n = m_n / C_n^{\text{gen}}$ (coincidence collapse factor)

**Pattern**: $\kappa_n$ decreases dramatically as $n$ increases, showing increasingly extreme geometric coincidence in the optimal configuration.

## Recurrence Implementation

Given the $m_n$ values above, computing $g(n)$ is trivial:

```python
def g(n, m_values):
    """Compute g(n) from precomputed m values."""
    return sum(m_values[0:n+1])
```
